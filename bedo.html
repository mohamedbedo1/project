  1. Electrical Engineeering:


    2. Power Engineering is a specialized branch of Electrical Engineering that focuses on the generation, transmission, and distribution of electrical power. It deals with large-scale systems such as power plants, transformers, transmission lines, and electrical grids. Engineers in this field ensure that electricity is delivered efficiently and safely to homes, businesses, and industries. They also work on integrating renewable energy sources like solar and wind into the power grid. Power Engineering is essential for maintaining the stability and reliability of modern energy systems.

  2. Communication & Computer enginnering:

    3. -Communication Engineering: Communication Engineering focuses on the transmission and reception of data over various types of media such as cables, satellites, and wireless networks. Engineers in this field work on designing and improving systems like mobile networks, internet infrastructure, and broadcasting. They deal with signals, modulation, antennas, and digital communication technologies. This specialization is essential in supporting modern communication systems like 5G and the Internet of Things (IoT). Communication engineers are in high demand in telecom companies, tech firms, and global industries.

    -Computer Engineering: Computer Engineering combines electrical engineering and computer science to design and develop computer systems and hardware. It involves working on processors, memory units, embedded systems, and digital logic circuits. Computer engineers also focus on integrating software and hardware to ensure smooth system performance. They contribute to the development of smart devices, robotics, and advanced computing technologies. This field plays a critical role in automation, artificial intelligence, and modern tech innovation.
